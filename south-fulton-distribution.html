The challenge

One schedule, three trades, zero slack.

A private industrial client needed a 42,000 sq ft distribution facility built in South Fulton, GA on a fixed 11-month schedule and $8.4M budget. Pre-engineered metal (PEM) erection, concrete flatwork, and full MEP rough-in all had to sequence under one schedule — any one trade slipping pushes the whole handover date.

The approach

One PM, one schedule everyone signs off on.

One project manager assigned at award — the same process every Big 7 commercial job runs: a weekly owner-schedule call with a two-week look-ahead, submittals logged and tracked, RFIs answered inside 48 hours (not chased six weeks later), and a close-out package — as-builts, O&M manuals, warranty binder — built in parallel with construction instead of assembled after the fact.

The result

Delivered on schedule, punch list closed early.

“Big 7 held the schedule down to the day and finished the punch list two weeks early. Every RFI answered inside 24 hours. That never happens.”

Marcus H., Director of Development
